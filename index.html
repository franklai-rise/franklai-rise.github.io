Selected Research

Master's Thesis · Ongoing

Raft-type Wave Energy Converter Using Decommissioned GFRP Blades

Proposed a novel solution to the global wind blade waste problem by repurposing decommissioned GFRP blades into wave energy converters (WEC). The research spans three sequential phases from material characterization to full system simulation.

Micro-CT Instron Abaqus ANSYS Python AQWA
Phase 1  —  Material Characterization

Analyzed damage mechanisms in service-aged GFRP materials using Micro-CT scanning and Instron tensile/compression testing to determine residual mechanical properties of decommissioned blade composites.

Phase 2  —  Statistical Damage Modeling

Established a stochastic damage model using Weibull distribution to predict failure probabilities of GFRP under varying load conditions, enabling reliability-based design.

Phase 3  —  System Design & Simulation

Designing GFRP-Steel connections and WEC geometry using Abaqus/ANSYS FEA. Hydrodynamic analysis in AQWA, followed by planned wave tank validation experiments.
